PORK ADOBO, ONE OF THE WELL KNOWN FOOD OF THE PHILIPPINES AND ONE OF THE WELL KNOWN FOOD THAT SIGMAS ENJOY

When you ask a foreigner to give one filipino food that they know, they are most likely to say adobo. Aren`t you mad? they know adobo while you don`t even know what it looks like? but fret not for I shall not only give you a glimpse of an adobo, I SHALL ALSO TEACH YOU HOW TO COOK ONE!

INGREDIENTS FOR OUR PORK ADOBO

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 pounds pork shoulder (cut into chunks)
  • ¼ cup cane vinegar or white vinegar
  • ⅓ cup low sodium soy sauce
  • 6 cloves garlic (chopped)
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 teaspoons black peppercorns
  • 2 teaspoons sugar
  • 2 cups water

STEPS TO DO TO MAKE OUR PORK ADOBO

  1. In a medium dutch oven or pot over medium high heat, add the oil and sear the pork until browned on all sides.
  2. Add the vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, bay leaf, peppercorns (tied in cheese cloth if desired), sugar, and water, and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat to medium low, cover, and simmer for 1 hour.
  3. Remove the cover and continue simmering for another 30 minutes to reduce the sauce.
  4. Serve over rice!
